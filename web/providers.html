WHAT MAKES A POLICY COVERABLE

Specific enough to verify.
Narrow enough to fund.

  1. 01

    A listed provider identity

    The service and provider wallet must be recoverable from public marketplace evidence.

  2. 02

    A published service boundary

    The promise names the work, required inputs, and exclusions instead of saying “good service.”

  3. 03

    An objective deadline

    The breach condition must be independently testable from the accepted-job state and clock.

  4. 04

    A supportable cap

    The job value, policy maximum, and uncommitted reserve determine the covenant—not provider marketing.
