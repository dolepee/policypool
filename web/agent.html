THE COVERAGE LOOP

No verified job, no covenant.
No reserve, no promise.

Three stages keep the product narrow and auditable. Subjective quality, forecasts, and caller-supplied policy overrides are outside the decision.

  1. 01

    Verify the accepted order

    Creation and acceptance events bind the buyer, provider, job, service hash, asset, and paid amount.

  2. 02

    Lock a bounded cap

    The registered policy supplies the SLA and maximum. Job value and uncommitted reserve narrow it further.

  3. 03

    Release or pay

    Delivery releases capacity. A verified missed deadline creates a bounded payout from the public reserve.

VERIFY WITHOUT TRUSTING THE UI

Four claims. Four independent checks.

The proof path starts with public OKX task state, follows X Layer transfers, and ends in a durable receipt. Fallback numbers are never shown when live reads fail.

01 / TARGET

The job was accepted.

The acceptance transaction is decoded from task escrow, not copied from the request.

02 / PAYMENT

The service call settled.

The receipt records a matching token transfer from the verified payer to the service recipient.

03 / SOLVENCY

The cap fits the reserve.

Atomic accounting exposes active, pending, payout-due, and uncommitted capacity.

04 / PAYOUT

Paid means paid.

The reserve transfer must match buyer, asset, and amount. An arbitrary transaction hash is rejected.
