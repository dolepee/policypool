  1. 01

    Verify the order

    The creation and acceptance transactions bind the buyer, job ID, target agent, provider wallet, asset, paid amount, service type, and exact accepted-service hash.

  2. 02

    Check published policy

    The target must have a registered policy snapshot. Caller-supplied policy overrides are ignored.

  3. 03

    Settle the service call

    A valid payment authorization is verified, settled on X Layer, and checked against the token Transfer event.

  4. 04

    Reserve the cap

    Active plus pending plus payout-due liabilities may never exceed the live public reserve.

  5. 05

    Read the deadline

    The reconciler reads the public job status. Only an accepted job still undelivered after its stored deadline breaches.

  6. 06

    Verify the payout

    A receipt becomes paid only after a matching reserve-to-buyer token transfer is found on X Layer.

WHAT THE RECEIPT PROVES

Four claims. Four independent checks.

TARGET

The job was accepted.

The acceptance transaction is decoded from the OKX task escrow, not copied from the request body.

PAYMENT

The API call settled.

The response records a real token transfer from the verified payer to the service recipient.

SOLVENCY

The cap fits the reserve.

Durable accounting reserves capacity atomically and exposes active, pending, and payout-due totals.

PAYOUT

Paid means paid.

A caller cannot attach an arbitrary transaction hash. The reserve transfer must match buyer, token, and amount.

The covered deadline comes from the registered target-policy SLA plus the verified acceptance block. Caller-supplied deadlines, payment status, policy overrides, breach flags, clocks, delivery hashes, and payout hashes are not trusted. Unknown targets are rejected before payment. The current release is an experimental software warranty layer with a capped public reserve, not protocol-native escrow or insurance.

THE FOUNDATION

PolicyPool started by making liquidity obey its own rules.

The original X Layer v4 hook proves the same primitive at a different execution boundary: publish a covenant, enforce it before capital moves, and emit a receipt. Agent Coverage carries that discipline into paid agent work.
